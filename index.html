MenuWords

Don't know your tagines from your sambars, or your carpaccios from your ceviches? MenuWords is here to help.

Report missing words to @MenuWords.

Words A-M

assiette

a variety of items served on a plate, as in “an assiette of deserts” or “an assiette of lamb”

ballotine

a de-boned thigh piece of poultry, stuffed with ground lean meat emulsified with fat, and poached, roasted or braised

bavarois also Crème Bavaroise or Bavarian Cream

a custard thickened with gelatin instead of flour/cornstarch, flavoured with liquer and often served with fruit sauce or purée

bearnnaise

a sauce made from clarified butter (butter from which the water and solids have been removed), egg yolks and white wine vinegar, flavoured with herbs

beignet

a pastry made from deep-fried choux paste

benedict as in Eggs Benedict

Eggs Benedict is a dish made by topping halves of an English muffin with poached eggs, ham or bacon, and Hollandaise sauce

bisque

a rich, thick and creamy soup, usually made from seafood (especially lobster) or vegetables

blanquette as in Blanquette de veau

a ragoût made from white meat (often veal) cooked in a sauce made from raw bones and chopped celery, onions and carrots, cream and egg yolks

bordelaise

a sauce made from dry red wine, bone marrow, butter, shallots and demi-glace (a rich brown sauce made from veal stock, vegetables and seasonings)

boulangère as in Boulangère Potatoes

Boulangère Potatoes are similar to Dauphinoise Potatoes, made from thinly sliced potatoes cooked with onions, sage or thyme, and vegetable stock

boullibaise

a Provençal stew made from fish and/or shellfish and/or seafood

braised also braising or pot roasting

a cooking method in which meat is first seared at high temperature and then finished in a covered pot containing a liquid chosen to impart a given flavour to the meat

brûlée as in Crème Brûlée

Crème Brûlée is a desert made from a custard base with a burnt sugar topping. It is usually vanilla flavoured but may include other flavours such as fruit or liquer.

canneloni

a cylindrical piece of pasta, filled and covered with a sauce and then baked

carpaccio

a dish of raw meat or fish served thinly sliced

ceviche also cebiche or seviche

a dish of raw fish marinated in citrus juices and spiced with chilli

chowder

a thickened seafood or vegetable soup

clafoutis also flaungnarde

A baked dessert, usually containing fruit (especially cherries) covered with a thick flan- or pancake-like batter

compote

a desert made from fruit in sugar syrup

confit

meat that has been deep-fried (often in its own fat) at low temperatures

consommé

a clear soup made from a rich stock from which the solids have been removed by the addition of egg whites

coulis

a thick sauce made from puréed fruit or vegetables, often served with desserts

croquette

a small package of mashed potato, meat, seafood, cheese or vegetables that has been covered in breadcrumbs and deep-fried

dauphinoise as in Dauphinoise Potatoes

Dauphinoise Potatoes is a dish made from thinly sliced potatoes served with a creamy garlicky sauce

florentine as in Eggs Florentine

a dish served with spinach
or an Italian biscuit made from nuts and candied cherries set in caramel

fondant

icing used to decorate or sculpt cakes or pastries
as in Fondant Potatoes: a rich and buttery potato dish

fricassée

a method of cooking meat in which the meat is chopped, sautéed and braised and served with a white sauce

galette

a flat and round crusty cake

granita

a semi-frozen dessert made from sugar and ice; not to be confused with sorbet

gremolata

a condiment made of chopped lemon zest, garlic and parsley; may also includes mint, rosemary, sage or anchovies

à la greque as in Mushrooms à la Greque

served in a sauce made from olive oil, lemon juice and seasonings

hollandaise

a sauce made from egg yolks, butter, lemon juice and white or cayenne pepper

jus

technically the juices created as a by-product of the cooking of meat dishes, but often used to refer to a rich, slightly reduced stock used as a sauce. It is often served with roasted meats.

à la lyonnaise

served with onions or caramelised onions

macerated

food that has been broken into pieces by the addition of liquid

à la meunière

fish served with brown butter, chopped parsley and lemon

milanese

of meats: served coated in breadcrumbs and browned in hot oil or butter
of pasta: served with a sauce of tomatoes, mushrooms, cheese, truffles and shredded meats

mille-feuille also vanilla slice, custard slice or Napoleon

A layered dessert made from puff pastry and crème pâtissière, often also including whipped cream or jam

à la minute

cooked to order, rather than prepared in advance
of sauces: prepared in the same pan in which the item it is served with was cooked

mousseline

a hollandaise sauce or mayonnaise to which whipped cream has been added

Words N-Z

niçoise as in Salad Niçoise

Salad Niçoise is a salad made from tuna, olives, hard-boiled eggs, anchovies, tomatoes and green beans, served with a vinaigrette dressing

parfait

a frozen dessert made from sugar syrup, egg and cream, often also containing alcohol
or a very smooth pâté made from liver and flavoured with liqeur

paysanne

very thinly cut vegetables

pâté

a mixture of cooked ground meat and fat that has been minced into a spreadable paste

pavé

any dish served in a slab-like fashion

pistou

a cold sauce made from garlic, basil and olive oil; it sometimes includes hard cheese such as parmesan or pecorino

provençal as in Provençal Vegetables

Provençal Vegetables is a selection of vegetables from the Provence region of southern France. Often contains tomatoes, aubergine, courgine, artichokes, asparagus, bell peppers, carrots, onions or garlic.

ragoût

a stew slow-cooked at low temperature

raviolli

a stuffed pasta dish made from two layers of thin pasta

rouille

a sauce made from olive oil, breadcrumbs, garlic, saffron and chillis

sarladaise as in Salardaise Potatoes

Salardaise Potatoes is a dish made from thin slices of potato cooked in goose or duck fat with parsley and garlic

sambar also sambhar or sambaaru

a vegetable stew based on a tamarind and pigeon pea broth

sautéed

a cooking method in which ingredients are quickly cooked at high temperatures in a small amount of oil or fat

sorbet

a dense icecream-like non-dairy dessert made from sweetened water and fruit flavourings; not to be confused with granita

soufflé

a lightly baked cake served as a savory main dish or (more commonly) as a sweet dessert

sous vide

cooked very slowly at very low temperature in an airtight plastic bag

sweetbreads also ris

a collective term for meats (most commonly veal, but also beef or pork) from the neck (throat or gullet sweetbreads) or the pancreas (stomach or heart sweetbreads) of an animal

tagine also tajine

a rich stew of meat or fish with vegetables or fruit

tapenade

a dish made from puréed or finely chopped olives, capers and anchovies with olive oil

tartare also tartar

finely chopped raw meat or fish, most commonly steak or tuna, often served with onions and capers

terrine

similar to pâté, but made with more coarsely chopped ingredients; a mixture of meat and fat minced into a spreadable paste

tortellini

ring-shaped stuffed pasta

velouté

a light stock (usually chicken or fish) that has been thickened with butter and flour

vinaigrette

a dressing, sauce or marinade made from oil and vinegar, often flavoured with herbs and spices
