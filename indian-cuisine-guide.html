Curries ↑

Balti

A medium-hot stir-fried curry made with peppers and coriander.

Bhuna

A medium-hot curry in a thick sauce with some vegetables.

Biryani

A dry dish in which spiced rice and meat are cooked together and served with a vegetable curry sauce.

Butter Chicken also Murgh Makhani

A mild curry made with garam masala, ginger, garlic, lemon/lime, coriander, cumin, tumeric and chillis.

Dhansak

A hot curry made with lentils, vegetables, cumin, ginger and garlic. Sometimes also includes pumpkin or pineapple.

Dopiaza also Dupiaza

A medium-hot curry made with a large amount of onion combined with ginger, garlic, cardamom and cloves.

Jalfrezi

A hot curry made with peppers, onions and lots of green chillis.

Kashmir also Malaya

A sweet curry made with raisins, pineapples, bananas and/or lychees.

Korai

Similar to a Balti; a hot or medium-hot stir-fried curry with tomatoes, peppers and onions.

Korma also Qorma / Corma / Kurma

A mild curry made with yoghurt, almond and coconut.

Madras

A hot or very hot version of the standard curry, made with extra chilli powder. Ingredients vary widely but often include tamarind, anise, ginger, paprika or turmeric.

Pasanda also Pasander

A mild curry made with almond, cream, coconut milk and cardamom. It is the best curry and you should try it.

Pathia also Patia

Similar to a Dhansak; a hot, sweet and sour curry made with chillis and vinegar.

Phall also Phal

A very hot curry made with tomatoes, ginger and fennel seeds. It is often made with scotch bonnet or habanero chillis.

Rogan Josh also Roghan Josh

A medium-hot curry made with tomatoes, paprika and coriander.

Tikka Masala

The archetypal British curry, allegedly created specifically for the UK market. It is usually a mild curry, but there is absolutely no agreement as to how it is prepared.

Vindaloo

A very hot curry, at least partially based on a Madras. Sometimes made with wine/wine vinegar and ginger, and available in even hotter versions called tindaloo or bindaloo.
